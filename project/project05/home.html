Korean BBQ

You are seriously missing out if you haven't experienced Korean BBQ yet! Here is a beginner's guide to ordering Korean BBQ like an expert if you are hesitant to give it a try.

What is Korean BBQ?

Korean BBQ is a famous technique in Korean cooking for grilling meat, usually pork and beef. What makes Korean BBQ interesting is that the food is prepared on charcoal grills that are built into each dining table. One of the advantages of having a built-in grill is that it keeps the meat warm and juicy. If you are a first-timer, you are in for a treat because that first bite will blow your mind! Any meat that came right out of the fire tastes the best. Another advantage of a built-in grill is that eating becomes exciting. Korean BBQ is all about the experience. Grilling together and sharing adds so much fun to the dining experience. If you have read this far and it is still difficult to picture what it’s like, think of it as an intimate and private hibachi experience.

Here are a few tips before going.

1. Do We Need to Cook for Ourselves?

Yes and no. Some restaurants will have staff to do the cooking for you, but some don’t. If they don’t provide a grilling service, you can simply grill for yourself. Don’t worry, the meat is served in an ‘easy-to- grill’ condition. If you are struggling, you can simply ask the server and they will show you how to grill it properly.

P.S. - Always feel free to ask the server to change the grill when it becomes too smoky.

2. Banchan and Side Dishes

Every Korean BBQ place will have Banchan and side dishes. Banchan is a complimentary selection of vegetable side dishes that mysteriously arrive right after placing an order. Yes, they are free and you can ask for more for free. Every restaurant serves slightly different Banchans but mostly you will find fresh lettuce, Kimchi, pickled leaves, Samjang sauce, scallion salad, and seasoned bean sprouts.

Unlike Banchan, side dishes will cost you money but it is highly recommended. Many people will usually order a couple of side dishes with their choices of meat. The most famous side dishes are kimchi-jjigae (stew), Doenjang-jjigae (stew), gyelanjjim (egg custard), naengmyeon (cold noodles), and fried rice.

3. Enjoy with Soju

As mentioned earlier, Korean BBQ is all about the experience. If you want to fully experience an authentic Korean vibe, try a bottle of Soju with your friends. Soju tastes like Vodka but much lighter. It goes very well with Korean BBQ because it cleanses your palate.
